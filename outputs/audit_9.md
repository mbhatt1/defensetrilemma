### 9. "1/66 paraphrases unsafe" is consistent with PAIR 0% ASR (no contradiction)

Verdict: ✅

Evidence:
- Paper lines 2161–2171 explicitly address this and argue consistency: "Within the 5-iteration budget the paraphrase wrapper blocks all ten behaviors (ASR drops from 1.00 to 0.00). This does *not* contradict Theorem 8.3's injective-incomplete horn: the paraphrase defense is injective on our 66-prompt sample (all 66 rewrites are distinct), and \Cref{tab:paraphrase-unsafe} already exhibits 1/66 paraphrases that yield $f(D(x)) > \tau$. The PAIR attacker with gpt-4o-mini as refiner and a 5-iteration budget is not sufficient to locate that bypass; a persistent attacker (more iterations, stronger refiner) is predicted to find one by \Cref{thm:multi-turn}'s multi-turn convergence argument."
- Table `tables/pair_baseline.tex` lines 17–23 restate the same reconciliation: 5-iter budget is "insufficient for the attacker to locate that bypass, consistent with the theorem's asymptotic (not per-iteration) claim."
- Logical check: 1/66 ≈ 1.5% per-paraphrase unsafe rate on the 66-prompt forced-collapse sample. PAIR baseline samples 10 seeds, refined over 5 iterations, so the attack never attempts the 66 static paraphrases; the probability that a gpt-4o-mini refiner stumbles onto a bypass within 5 iterations per behavior is plausibly small. No contradiction — the claims live in distinct samples and describe different quantities (prevalence vs. finite-budget attacker success).
